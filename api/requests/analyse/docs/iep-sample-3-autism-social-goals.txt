PRESENT LEVELS OF ACADEMIC ACHIEVEMENT AND FUNCTIONAL PERFORMANCE

Academic Performance:
Emma performs at or above grade level in all academic areas. She excels in mathematics and science, demonstrating strong analytical and problem-solving skills. Reading comprehension and written expression are also strengths, with Emma performing at the 8th grade level in these areas.

Social-Emotional Functioning:
Emma experiences significant difficulty with social interactions and transitions. She has limited understanding of social cues and struggles to initiate or maintain conversations with peers. Changes in routine cause significant anxiety, leading to emotional dysregulation that can manifest as withdrawal, verbal outbursts, or refusal to participate in activities.

Emma demonstrates difficulty with:
- Interpreting facial expressions and body language (currently at 30% accuracy)
- Initiating conversations with peers (0-1 initiations per day)
- Maintaining flexible thinking during unexpected changes (requires adult support in 90% of transitions)
- Self-regulation when experiencing anxiety (uses coping strategies independently only 20% of the time)

ANNUAL GOALS

Goal 1: Social Communication
By August 20, 2026, Emma will initiate appropriate conversations with peers using a conversation starter strategy with 80% success, improving from her current baseline of 0-1 initiations per day, as measured by daily data collection and weekly communication logs.

Baseline: 0-1 peer-initiated conversations per day (August 2025)
Target: 3-4 peer-initiated conversations per day with 80% appropriateness
Measurement Method: Daily data collection by classroom staff, weekly communication logs
Criteria for Success: Average of 3-4 appropriate peer initiations per day over 2 consecutive weeks
Progress Reporting Frequency: Weekly

Goal 2: Emotional Regulation
By August 20, 2026, Emma will independently use at least 2 coping strategies when experiencing anxiety or frustration, increasing from her current 20% independence to 70% independence, as measured by behavior tracking logs and self-monitoring checklists.

Baseline: Uses coping strategies independently 20% of the time when anxious/frustrated
Target: Uses coping strategies independently 70% of the time
Measurement Method: Daily behavior tracking, self-monitoring checklists, teacher observation
Criteria for Success: 70% or higher independent use over 3 consecutive weeks
Progress Reporting Frequency: Bi-weekly

Goal 3: Flexible Thinking
By August 20, 2026, Emma will demonstrate flexible thinking by accepting changes to routines or unexpected events with minimal adult support in 75% of transition situations, improving from her current 10% independence, as measured by daily transition logs.

Baseline: Accepts changes independently in 10% of transition situations (August 2025)
Target: Accepts changes independently in 75% of situations
Measurement Method: Daily transition logs, behavior incident reports
Criteria for Success: 75% or higher independence across all transitions for 4 consecutive weeks
Progress Reporting Frequency: Weekly

Goal 4: Perspective Taking
By August 20, 2026, Emma will identify emotions of others based on facial expressions, body language, and situational context with 75% accuracy, improving from her current 30% accuracy, as measured by structured social scenarios and classroom observations.

Baseline: 30% accuracy identifying others' emotions (August 2025)
Target: 75% accuracy
Measurement Method: Weekly structured social scenario assessments, classroom observation data
Criteria for Success: 75% or higher accuracy on 4 consecutive weekly assessments
Progress Reporting Frequency: Weekly

ACCOMMODATIONS AND MODIFICATIONS

Classroom Accommodations:
- Visual schedule provided daily with advance notice of any changes
- 5-minute warning before all transitions
- Access to quiet space for self-regulation breaks (up to 10 minutes, 3 times per day as needed)
- Social scripts and conversation prompts available
- Modified group work expectations (smaller groups of 2-3 students, with defined roles)
- Extended time for processing verbal instructions (minimum 10 seconds wait time)

Testing Accommodations:
- Extended time (time and a half) for tests
- Separate, quiet testing location
- Breaks during testing (every 20 minutes or as needed)
- Use of fidget tools during testing

Social-Emotional Supports:
- Daily check-in with assigned staff member (5 minutes at start of day)
- Peer buddy system for lunch and unstructured times
- Pre-teaching of social expectations for new activities
- Visual cue cards for emotional regulation strategies

BEHAVIORAL INTERVENTION PLAN

Emma has a Behavioral Intervention Plan (BIP) that addresses anxiety-related behaviors and provides positive behavioral supports. The BIP includes:

Target Behaviors:
1. Withdrawal/refusal to participate during transitions or unexpected changes
2. Verbal outbursts when experiencing frustration or anxiety

Replacement Behaviors:
1. Use of "break card" to request self-regulation time
2. Use of verbal scripts to express frustration appropriately

Prevention Strategies:
- Visual schedules with advance notice of changes
- Pre-teaching and rehearsal of new routines
- Scheduled self-regulation breaks

Teaching Strategies:
- Direct instruction in emotional regulation techniques
- Social stories for common anxiety-producing situations
- Role-playing and practice with coping strategies

Response Strategies:
- Calm, neutral redirection to coping strategies
- Access to quiet space for de-escalation
- Debriefing after incidents to identify triggers and effective strategies

LEAST RESTRICTIVE ENVIRONMENT

Emma will spend 92% of her school day in the general education classroom with same-age peers. She will receive social skills instruction in a small group setting (resource room) for 90 minutes per week (approximately 4% of the school day) and individual counseling for 60 minutes per week (approximately 3% of the school day).

Emma participates in all general education classes, including core academic subjects, electives (currently enrolled in Art and Computer Science), lunch, and all school assemblies and activities. She has full access to extracurricular activities with supports as needed.

EXTENDED SCHOOL YEAR (ESY)
