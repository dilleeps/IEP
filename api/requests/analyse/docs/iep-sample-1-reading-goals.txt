PRESENT LEVELS OF ACADEMIC ACHIEVEMENT AND FUNCTIONAL PERFORMANCE (PLAAFP)

Reading:
Alex currently reads at a 2nd grade level on the Fountas & Pinnell assessment. He demonstrates difficulty with phonemic awareness, decoding multisyllabic words, and reading comprehension. When presented with grade-level text, Alex requires repeated prompting and can only answer literal comprehension questions with 40% accuracy. His oral reading fluency is 45 words per minute with 75% accuracy, compared to the 4th grade benchmark of 120 words per minute with 95% accuracy.

Math:
Alex performs at grade level in mathematics. He demonstrates strong computational skills and can solve multi-step word problems with 85% accuracy when the text is read aloud to him.

ANNUAL GOALS

Goal 1: Reading Fluency
By September 15, 2026, Alex will increase his oral reading fluency from 45 words correct per minute (wcpm) to 90 wcpm on 4th grade level passages, as measured by DIBELS assessments conducted monthly.

Baseline: 45 wcpm with 75% accuracy (September 2025)
Target: 90 wcpm with 90% accuracy
Measurement Method: DIBELS Next Oral Reading Fluency probes
Criteria for Success: 90 wcpm with 90% accuracy on 3 consecutive probes
Progress Reporting Frequency: Monthly

Goal 2: Reading Comprehension
By September 15, 2026, Alex will answer literal and inferential comprehension questions about 4th grade level texts with 80% accuracy, up from his current 40% accuracy, as measured by teacher-created assessments and Reading A-Z comprehension quizzes administered bi-weekly.

Baseline: 40% accuracy on grade-level comprehension questions (September 2025)
Target: 80% accuracy
Measurement Method: Teacher-created comprehension assessments and Reading A-Z quizzes
Criteria for Success: 80% or higher on 4 consecutive assessments
Progress Reporting Frequency: Bi-weekly

Goal 3: Phonemic Awareness and Decoding
By September 15, 2026, Alex will decode multisyllabic words (3+ syllables) with 85% accuracy, improving from his current 45% accuracy, using syllable division strategies, as measured by weekly word lists and curriculum-based assessments.

Baseline: 45% accuracy decoding multisyllabic words (September 2025)
Target: 85% accuracy
Measurement Method: Weekly word lists (20 words each) and curriculum-based assessments
Criteria for Success: 85% or higher accuracy on 3 consecutive weekly assessments
Progress Reporting Frequency: Weekly

ACCOMMODATIONS AND MODIFICATIONS

Testing Accommodations:
- Extended time (time and a half) on all tests and quizzes
- Tests read aloud for all subjects except reading fluency assessments
- Frequent breaks during testing (every 15 minutes)
- Small group or separate setting for standardized tests

Classroom Accommodations:
- Preferential seating near teacher and away from distractions
- Copy of teacher notes or guided note-taking templates
- Audio books for content area subjects
- Use of text-to-speech software for assignments
- Reduced homework load (50% reduction) with focus on quality over quantity
- Visual schedules and checklists for multi-step tasks

LEAST RESTRICTIVE ENVIRONMENT (LRE)

Alex will spend 75% of his school day in the general education classroom with same-age peers. He will receive specialized reading instruction in the resource room for 45 minutes daily (approximately 12% of the school day) and speech-language therapy services in a separate location for 30 minutes twice weekly (approximately 4% of the school day).

The remaining time (approximately 9%) includes lunch, recess, and transitions between settings. Alex participates in all general education activities including physical education, music, art, and lunch/recess with his non-disabled peers.

PROGRESS REPORTING

Progress toward annual goals will be reported to parents:
- Monthly for Goal 1 (Reading Fluency)
- Bi-weekly for Goal 2 (Reading Comprehension)
- Weekly for Goal 3 (Phonemic Awareness)

Progress reports will be provided using the following scale:
- Mastered: Student has achieved the annual goal
- Sufficient Progress: Student is making expected progress toward annual goal
- Insufficient Progress: Student is not making expected progress and instructional strategies need to be adjusted
- Regression: Student has lost previously acquired skills
