PRESENT LEVELS OF ACADEMIC ACHIEVEMENT AND FUNCTIONAL PERFORMANCE

Academic Performance:
Michael performs at grade level in reading and mathematics. He demonstrates strong visual learning skills and excels in subjects that incorporate hands-on activities.

Speech and Language:
Michael presents with a moderate articulation disorder affecting multiple speech sounds including /r/, /s/, /l/, and /th/. His speech intelligibility is approximately 65% to unfamiliar listeners and 85% to familiar listeners. These articulation errors impact his ability to communicate effectively with peers and adults, and he sometimes avoids verbal participation in class due to awareness of his speech differences.

Current speech sound production accuracy:
- /r/ sound: 20% accuracy in all positions
- /s/ sound: 40% accuracy in all positions  
- /l/ sound: 35% accuracy in initial position, 15% in final position
- /th/ sound: 10% accuracy in all positions

Receptive and expressive language skills are within normal limits. Michael follows multi-step directions, uses age-appropriate vocabulary, and constructs grammatically correct sentences.

Social-Emotional Impact:
Michael has begun to show reluctance to speak in front of the class or participate in oral reading activities. Teacher reports indicate he is withdrawing from group discussions and sometimes uses gestures instead of words when communicating with peers.

ANNUAL GOALS

Goal 1: Articulation - /r/ Sound
By October 5, 2026, Michael will produce the /r/ sound in all positions of words (initial, medial, final) with 80% accuracy across 3 consecutive speech therapy sessions, improving from his current 20% accuracy, as measured by articulation probes and conversational speech samples.

Baseline: 20% accuracy for /r/ in all positions (October 2025)
Target: 80% accuracy in all positions
Measurement Method: Weekly articulation probes (20 words per probe), conversational speech samples
Criteria for Success: 80% or higher accuracy on 3 consecutive weekly probes
Progress Reporting Frequency: Bi-weekly

Goal 2: Articulation - /s/ Sound  
By October 5, 2026, Michael will produce the /s/ sound in all positions of words with 85% accuracy in structured sentences and 75% accuracy in conversational speech, improving from his current 40% accuracy, as measured by articulation assessments and speech samples.

Baseline: 40% accuracy for /s/ in all positions (October 2025)
Target: 85% accuracy in structured sentences, 75% in conversation
Measurement Method: Weekly articulation probes, conversational speech samples (5 minutes)
Criteria for Success: Target accuracy maintained over 3 consecutive sessions
Progress Reporting Frequency: Bi-weekly

Goal 3: Articulation - /l/ Sound
By October 5, 2026, Michael will produce the /l/ sound with 80% accuracy in initial position and 70% accuracy in final position of words during structured activities, improving from his current 35% initial and 15% final position accuracy, as measured by weekly speech probes.

Baseline: 35% accuracy initial position, 15% accuracy final position (October 2025)
Target: 80% accuracy initial position, 70% accuracy final position  
Measurement Method: Weekly articulation probes (10 words each position)
Criteria for Success: Target accuracy on 3 consecutive weekly probes
Progress Reporting Frequency: Bi-weekly

Goal 4: Communication Confidence
By October 5, 2026, Michael will verbally participate in classroom activities (such as answering questions, sharing during circle time, and participating in group work) at least 5 times per day, up from his current average of 1-2 times per day, as measured by teacher observation logs and self-monitoring checklists.

Baseline: 1-2 verbal participations per day (October 2025)
Target: 5 or more verbal participations per day
Measurement Method: Daily teacher observation log, weekly student self-monitoring
Criteria for Success: Average of 5+ participations per day over 2 consecutive weeks
Progress Reporting Frequency: Weekly

SPECIAL EDUCATION AND RELATED SERVICES

Speech-Language Therapy:
- Service Type: Speech-Language Therapy
- Provider: Speech-Language Pathologist (Ms. Amanda Foster, CCC-SLP)
- Location: Speech Therapy Room
- Service Delivery Model: Direct therapy (individual and small group)
- Frequency: 3 times per week
- Duration: 30 minutes per session
- Total Weekly Minutes: 90 minutes
- Start Date: October 10, 2025
- End Date: October 9, 2026

Session Structure:
- Individual sessions: 2x per week (30 minutes each) - focused articulation practice
- Small group session: 1x per week (30 minutes) - generalization and social communication

Consultation:
- Speech-Language Pathologist will consult with classroom teacher monthly (30 minutes) to provide strategies for supporting speech sound production in the classroom setting and encouraging verbal participation

ACCOMMODATIONS AND MODIFICATIONS

Classroom Accommodations:
- Allow extra time for verbal responses (minimum 10 seconds processing time)
- Provide opportunities for Michael to practice responses before speaking in front of class
- Offer alternative response methods when appropriate (written, drawing) while continuing to encourage verbal participation
- Seat near supportive peers who model appropriate communication
- Private praise for verbal participation efforts
- Small group settings for oral presentations initially, progressing to larger groups

Testing Accommodations:
- Allow verbal or written responses for assessment (Michael's choice)
- Extended time for tests requiring verbal responses
- Small group or individual testing setting if requested

Social-Emotional Supports:
- Positive reinforcement for all verbal communication attempts
- Weekly check-ins with speech therapist about feelings regarding speech
- Peer education (with parent consent) about speech differences to promote understanding and inclusion

CLASSROOM STRATEGIES FOR SPEECH SOUND PRACTICE

The speech-language pathologist will provide the classroom teacher with:
- List of target words for Michael's speech sounds
- Visual cues and reminders for correct sound production
- Suggestions for incorporating speech practice into classroom activities
- Strategies for gentle, positive correction without drawing negative attention

LEAST RESTRICTIVE ENVIRONMENT

Michael will spend 98% of his school day in the general education classroom with same-age peers. He will receive speech-language therapy services in a separate location for 90 minutes per week (approximately 2% of school day).

Michael participates in all general education activities including academic instruction, physical education, music, art, lunch, and recess with non-disabled peers. His speech therapy is scheduled during times that minimize removal from core academic instruction.

PROGRESS REPORTING

Progress toward annual goals will be reported to parents:
- Bi-weekly for Goals 1, 2, and 3 (Articulation goals)
- Weekly for Goal 4 (Communication Confidence)

Progress reports will include:
- Current accuracy percentages for each target sound
- Speech samples or recordings (with parent consent)
- Teacher feedback on classroom participation
- Recommendations for home practice

Parents will receive detailed quarterly progress reports and are encouraged to communicate with the speech therapist via email or phone for updates between formal reports.

HOME PROGRAM

The speech-language pathologist will provide:
- Weekly home practice activities (5-10 minutes per day, 5 days per week)
- List of target words for practice
- Visual cue cards for correct sound production
- Suggestions for incorporating speech practice into daily routines
- Video demonstrations of target sounds (if requested)

Parents are encouraged but not required to participate in home practice. All home activities will be designed to be fun, game-based, and appropriate for family participation.

PARTICIPATION IN STATE AND DISTRICT ASSESSMENTS

Michael will participate in all state and district assessments with the following accommodations:
- Extended time
- Small group or separate setting (if preferred)
- Text-to-speech for reading directions (if needed)
- Verbal or written responses (Michael's choice)

Michael does not require alternate assessments.
